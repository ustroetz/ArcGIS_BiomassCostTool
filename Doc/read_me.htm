*How to determine Distance?

Option 1 Google Maps:

Use the identify tool and click on the destination feature. Change in the Location field the Coordinates to Degrees Minutes Seconds and copy the Location. Go to Google Maps, Click the Get Directions Tab and paste in �A� the Coordinates. Go back to ArcMap and identify the first Access Point and copy the location and insert it into Google Map under B. Now hit the �Get Directions� button and copy the Distance into the DISTANCE_DESTINATION field in the table of attributes of the Access Points Features. Repeat these steps with all the Access Points.

Option 2 Find Route in ArcMap:

1. Open ArcMap.

2. Add the the streets.rs file, located in the \streetmap_na\data folder on the ESRI Data & Maps

and StreetMap North America DVD.

3. Add the Destination Feature Class and the Access Points to operable Areas.

4. Make sure, the Destination Feature and the Access Points to the operable Areas are on or

close to the streets in the layer.

4. Open the Find Route dialog box. (click the Find Route button on the Tools toolbar).

5. In the Stop Tab, click Get Point � Get Point Features, choose the Destination

�Feature Class containing the Access Point to the operable Areas and add the Stop.

6. Close the Find Route dialog box and select the first point on the Access Points Layer

7. Open the Find Route dialog box up again. Click in the Stops Tab Get Point � Get Feature

Points, choose the Access Points Layer, click Get Selected Points, and add the Stops.

8. Click Find Route.

9. Insert the driving distance into the DISTANCE_DESTINATION field of the prober point.

10. Repeat these steps for all the points in the Access Points Layer.

 

Option 3 Measure tool:

Insert a road layer and measure the distances between the Access Points and the Destination Feature with the Measure tool . Insert the distance then into the DISTANCE_DESTINATION field of the prober point.
